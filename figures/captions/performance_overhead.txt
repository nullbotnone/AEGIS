Combined performance summary from the real measurements. The left panel reports three direct-probe overhead views for each syscall family: elapsed-time overhead, task-clock overhead, and throughput loss. File-centric syscalls remain the worst case, with elapsed overhead of 24.0\% for \texttt{openat} and 28.3\% for \texttt{read}, while \texttt{connect} remains low at 4.04\%. The right panel shows end-to-end detection latency and verifier CPU overhead versus attestation interval; latency rises from 50.2\,ms at 100\,ms intervals to 500.2\,ms at 1\,s and 5.00\,s at 10\,s, while CPU overhead falls from 0.439\% to 0.039\% and 0.0039\%, respectively.
